About Cats

General Facts

  • Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping.
  • The oldest pet cat lived to be 38 years old.
  • Cats can make over 100 sounds.
  • A group of cats is called a clowder.
  • Cats have five toes on their front paws, four on the back.
  • Cats can run up to 30 mph.
  • There are over 500 million domestic cats in the world.
  • Cats have been domesticated for around 4,000 years.
  • The average domestic cat weighs about 10 pounds.

Physical Facts

  • A cat’s brain is 90% similar to a human’s brain.
  • Cats have three eyelids.
  • A cat’s heart beats 110-140 times per minute.
  • Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
  • Cats can jump up to six times their body length.
  • Cats have a reflective layer in their eyes called the tapetum lucidum.
  • Cats have over 20 muscles that control their ears.
  • Cats have 30 teeth.
  • A cat's jaw can't move sideways.
  • Cats have a specialized collarbone that allows them to always land on their feet.
  • Adult cats have 30 vertebrae.
  • A cat’s nose has around 200 million scent receptors.
  • Cats have whiskers on the backs of their front legs as well.

Historical and Cultural Facts

  • Black cats are good luck in Japan and the UK.
  • Ancient Egyptians worshipped cats.
  • The first cat in space was Félicette in 1963.
  • The world’s richest cat, Blackie, inherited $13 million.
  • Some cats have found their way home from over 500 miles away.
  • In ancient Egypt, killing a cat was punishable by death.
  • In the Middle Ages, cats were associated with witchcraft.
  • Many sailors kept cats on ships to control rodents and as good luck charms.
  • Abraham Lincoln kept four cats in the White House.
  • Ernest Hemingway had a famous collection of polydactyl (extra-toed) cats.

Behavioral Facts

  • Cats have a “righting reflex” to land on their feet.
  • Adult cats meow to communicate with humans, not other cats.
  • Cats use a variety of vocalizations to communicate with their humans.
  • Cats spend 30-50% of their waking hours grooming.
  • Cats knead with their paws to show contentment.
  • Cats rub against people to mark their territory.
  • Cats use their tails for balance and communication.
  • Cats can be trained to use a human toilet.
  • Cats often purr when they're content, but also when they're in pain or afraid.
  • Cats have the ability to purr at a frequency that can promote healing and reduce pain.
