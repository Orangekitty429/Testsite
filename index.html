About Cats

General Facts

  • Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping.
  • The oldest pet cat lived to be 38 years old.
  • Cats can make over 100 sounds.
  • A group of cats is called a clowder.
  • Cats have five toes on their front paws, four on the back.
  • Cats can run up to 30 mph.

Physical Facts

  • A cat’s brain is 90% similar to a human’s brain.
  • Cats have three eyelids.
  • A cat’s heart beats 110-140 times per minute.
  • Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
  • Cats can jump up to six times their body length.
  • Cats have a reflective layer in their eyes called the tapetum lucidum.

Historical and Cultural Facts

  • Black cats are good luck in Japan and the UK.
  • Ancient Egyptians worshipped cats.
  • The first cat in space was Félicette in 1963.
  • The world’s richest cat, Blackie, inherited $13 million.
  • Some cats have found their way home from over 500 miles away.

Behavioral Facts

  • Cats have a “righting reflex” to land on their feet.
  • Adult cats meow to communicate with humans, not other cats.
  • Cats use a variety of vocalizations to communicate with their humans.
  • Cats spend 30-50% of their waking hours grooming.

Communication

Cats use purrs, meows, and chirps to communicate with humans.

Toe Beans

The soft pads on a cat's paws are called "toe beans."

Ancient Pets

Cats were domesticated around 9,000 years ago in the Near East.

Purring Power

Cats purr to self-soothe and heal.

Unique Noseprints

Each cat's noseprint is unique, like a human fingerprint.

Curious Climbers

Cats have retractable claws for climbing and catching prey.

Night Vision

Cats have excellent night vision and can see in nearly total darkness.

Feline Flexibility

Cats are very flexible, with 230 to 250 bones in their bodies.

Tail Tales

A cat's tail helps with balance and communication.
